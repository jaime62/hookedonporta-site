Best Times for Surf Fishing

Twilight hours—early morning and evening—are money, especially when they align with a tide turn. With today’s tides (below), plan for a bump around the pre‑dawn high and again as water falls into the afternoon low.

  • Dawn: Work the first and second guts with live shrimp or croaker; slide to the jetties as current picks up.
  • Afternoon push: Hit the turn with spoons, shrimp, or finger mullet; bump leader for smacks/kings near rocks.
  • Evening: Target points and cuts as water drains—topwaters and soft plastics shine if winds stay light.

Offshore & Nearshore Species Targeting

Nearshore & Surf Reports

TPWD Port Aransas weekly report (Aug 27, 2025):

  • Redfish are good on the north & south jetties with live shrimp and cut or live mullet; oversize reds are great on cut crab or mullet.
  • Speckled trout are great freelined with croaker or shrimp along jetty rocks; reds & trout also great in the surf on live shrimp/croaker.
  • King mackerel & Spanish mackerel are great along the beach and jetties on ribbonfish or trolled shiny spoons/plugs. Pompano fair in the surf.

Summer Offshore Highlights

From June through August:

  • Red snapper: offshore bite strong; see season notes below.
  • King mackerel (kingfish): thick around shrimp boats and nearshore structure—drift ribbonfish or troll.
  • Mahi‑Mahi: target weedlines and color changes.
  • Billfish (sailfish/marlin): best with sustained blue water and warm temps.
