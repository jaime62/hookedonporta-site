Best Times for Surf Fishing

Twilight hours—early morning and evening—are money, especially when they align with a tide turn. With today’s tides (below), plan for a bump around the morning high and again as water falls into the evening low.

  • Morning push: Work the first and second guts with live shrimp or croaker; slide to the jetties as current picks up.
  • Afternoon into evening: Hit the turn with spoons, shrimp, or finger mullet; bump leader for smacks/kings near rocks.
  • Low-light: Target points and cuts as water drains—topwaters and soft plastics shine if winds stay light.

Offshore & Nearshore Species Targeting

Nearshore & Surf Reports

TPWD Port Aransas weekly report (Sep 3, 2025):

  • Redfish are good on the north & south jetties with live shrimp and cut or live mullet; oversize reds are great on cut crab or mullet.
  • Speckled trout are good freelined with croaker or shrimp along jetty rocks; reds & trout also great in the surf on croaker or live shrimp.
  • King mackerel & Spanish mackerel are fair to great along the beach and jetties on ribbonfish or trolled shiny spoons/plugs. Pompano fair in the surf.

Late‑Summer Offshore Highlights

  • Red snapper: consistent offshore bite; state waters open year‑round (see limits below).
  • King mackerel (kingfish): thick around shrimp boats and nearshore structure—drift ribbonfish or troll divers/spoons.
  • Mahi‑Mahi: target weedlines and color changes when blue water pushes close.
  • Billfish (sailfish/marlin): best with sustained blue water and warm temps.

Regulatory Seasons & Limits*

  • Red Snapper:
    • Federal waters (for‑hire/charter 2025): June 1 – Sept 16 (12:01 a.m.).
    • Federal waters (private anglers): Texas‑managed dates; always confirm current status. Federal bag: 2/day, 16″ min when applicable.
    • Texas state waters (0–9 nm): Open year‑round, 4/day, 15″ min.
  • Flounder: 5/day, 15″ min; fishery closed Nov 1 – Dec 14 annually.

Pro Tip: Always carry a saltwater license with your saltwater endorsement.
