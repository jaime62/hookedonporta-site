Best Times for Surf Fishing

If you're chasing fish from the shoreline, twilight hours—early morning and evening—are your golden window. Pair them with moving water (around high-to-falling tides or a strong tidal push) for the best shot.

This week’s easy planning cues for Port Aransas:

  • Mon (Aug 11): Sunrise ~6:56 AM, Sunset ~8:10 PM. Expect a morning high near sunrise, a midday low, then an afternoon high (fish around the tide turns).
  • Tue (Aug 12): Sunrise ~6:56 AM, Sunset ~8:10 PM. Similar pattern: early high followed by a late afternoon high.
  • Wed (Aug 13): Sunrise ~6:55–6:56 AM, Sunset ~8:10–8:11 PM. Aim for dawn and the afternoon tide push.

Plan to hit the beach at first light and again near dusk, especially as tides turn.

Offshore & Nearshore Species Targeting

Nearshore & Surf Reports

Latest TPWD weekly report (Aug 6):

  • Redfish are good on the north and south jetties with live shrimp and finger mullet; oversize reds taking cut crab or mullet.
  • Speckled trout are great freelined with croaker or shrimp along jetty rocks; trout and reds also good in the surf on live shrimp/croaker.
  • Kingfish & Spanish mackerel are great on ribbonfish or by trolling shiny spoons/plugs along the beach and jetties.

Summer Offshore Highlights

  • Red snapper: federal season opened June 1; state waters are open year-round. See limits below.
  • King mackerel (kingfish): thick around shrimp boats and nearshore structure; drift ribbonfish or troll.
  • Mahi-Mahi: work weedlines and color changes offshore.
  • Billfish (sailfish/marlin): best with warm water and long blue-water runs.

Regulatory Seasons & Limits*

  • Red Snapper:
    • Federal waters (private anglers): Opened June 1, 2025. Bag limit 2/day, 16″ min. Closing date is set by in-season monitoring.
    • Federal waters (for-hire vessels with federal permits): 2025 season runs June 1 – Sept 16, 2025 (12:01 a.m.).
    • State waters (0–9 nm): Open year-round. Bag limit 4/day, 15″ min.
  • Flounder: 5/day, 15″ min (valid through Aug 31, 2025); season closed Nov 1 – Dec 14.

Pro Tip: Always carry a saltwater license with your saltwater endorsement.
