Best Times for Surf Fishing

If you're chasing fish from the shoreline, twilight hours—early morning and evening—are your golden window, especially when they align with major or minor solunar feeding periods. Look out for these peaks when the moon is rising or setting or directly overhead.

According to today’s fishing forecast:

  • Thursday (Aug 7): Good fishing between 7:19 AM and 11:19 AM
  • Friday (Aug 8): Below average, but still workable between 9:09 AM and 1:09 PM
  • Monday (Aug 11): Great day with prime time early—4:03 AM to 8:03 AM

Plan to hit the beach during those early-morning windows for your best chances at surf success.

Offshore & Nearshore Species Targeting

Nearshore & Surf Reports

TPWD reports for August 6 show:

  • Redfish are doing great on the north and south jetties using live shrimp and finger mullet—and some monster reds are taking cut crab and mullet.
  • Speckled trout are hitting croaker and shrimp fished along rocks.
  • Kingfish and Spanish mackerel are showing up in force using ribbonfish or trolling shiny lures.

Summer Offshore Highlights

From June through August:

  • Red snapper is in-season—especially offshore—with a daily limit of 2 per person (16″ min) federally and 4 per day (15″ min) in state waters.
  • King mackerel (kingfish) are at peak numbers around shrimp boats; use shrimp as bait.
  • Mahi-Mahi thrive near weed lines—cruise days offer great hits.
  • Marlin and sailfish bite best in warm summer months—plan charters accordingly.

Regulatory Seasons & Limits*

  • Red Snapper:
    • Federal waters: Opens June 1, 2025 (2 fish/day, 16″ min). Closing is based on monitoring.
    • State waters: Open year-round (4 fish/day, 15″ min).
  • Flounder: Bag limit is 5/day (15″ min) until August 31, 2025. Closed from Nov 1 to Dec 14.

Pro Tip: Always carry a saltwater license with your saltwater endorsement. Exceptions apply (e.g., Free Fishing Day on June 7 or fishing from state park piers).

*Check current TPWD regulations and bag limits, as they may have changed since the time of this report.

Final Cast

Whether you're casting from the surf at dawn or chasing offshore giants, Port Aransas is delivering right now. Here's the game plan:

  • Hit the surf around dawn/evening when tides align with solunar peaks.
