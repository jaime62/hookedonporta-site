Best Times for Surf Fishing

If you're chasing fish from the shoreline, twilight hours—early morning and evening—are your golden window, especially when they align with moving water around tide turns.

  • Dawn: Work the first and second guts with live shrimp or croaker; move to the jetties as current picks up.
  • Afternoon tide push: Fish the turn with spoons, shrimp, or finger mullet; bump leader size for smacks/kings near rocks.
  • Evening: Target points and cuts as water drains—topwaters and soft plastics can shine if winds stay light.

Offshore & Nearshore Species Targeting

Nearshore & Surf Reports

Latest TPWD Port Aransas weekly report (Aug 20, 2025):

  • Redfish are good on the north & south jetties with live shrimp and finger mullet; oversize reds great on cut crab or mullet.
  • Speckled trout are great freelined with croaker or shrimp along jetty rocks; trout & reds also great in the surf on live shrimp/croaker.
  • King mackerel & Spanish mackerel are great on ribbonfish or by trolling shiny spoons/plugs along the beach and jetties.

Summer Offshore Highlights

From June through August:

  • Red snapper: offshore bite strong; see season notes below.
  • King mackerel (kingfish): thick around shrimp boats and nearshore structure—drift ribbonfish or troll.
  • Mahi‑Mahi: target weedlines and color changes.
  • Billfish (sailfish/marlin): best with sustained blue water and warm temps.

Regulatory Seasons & Limits*

  • Red Snapper:
    • Federal waters (for‑hire/charter): June 1 – Sept 16, 2025 (12:01 a.m. local).

    • State waters (0–9 nm): Open year‑round.

Final Cast

  • Work dawn/dusk windows and tide turns on the beach.
  • Jetty playbook: shrimp, croaker, and mullet for trout & reds; ribbonfish or shiny plugs for kings and smacks.
  • Offshore: snapper, kings, mahi—watch weather and weedlines.
  • Stay legal: double‑check bag & size limits and federal vs. state snapper rules.
